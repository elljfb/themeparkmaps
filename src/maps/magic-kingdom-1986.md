This 1986 guide map of the **Magic Kingdom** at Walt Disney World Resort captures the park during a decade of immense growth and classic 1980s aesthetic. With EPCOT Center now fully operational next door, the Magic Kingdom remained the premier fantasy destination, balancing time-honored characters and groundbreaking attractions.

![Magic Kingdom Map 1986](/assets/img/maps/magic_kingdom_map_1986.jpg)

### Classic Lands of 1986

At this point in its history, the Magic Kingdom consisted of six themed lands:

- **Main Street, U.S.A.** — the nostalgia-laden turn-of-the-century entrance path.
- **Adventureland** — home of the Jungle Cruise, Swiss Family Treehouse, and the swashbuckling **Pirates of the Caribbean**.
- **Frontierland** — anchored by the runaway mine train **Big Thunder Mountain Railroad** (which opened in 1980) and the Country Bear Jamboree.
- **Liberty Square** — the historic American area containing the classic **Haunted Mansion** and the Hall of Presidents.
- **Fantasyland** — featuring the original configuration of 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea: Submarine Voyage, Mr. Toad's Wild Ride, and Snow White's Scary Adventures.
- **Tomorrowland** — anchored by the gleaming white structure of **Space Mountain**, Carousel of Progress, and the Star Jets.

### Notable Nostalgia

- **Mickey's Birthdayland** (which later became Mickey's Starland, then Toontown) was not yet created; it would be introduced in 1988 for Mickey Mouse's 60th birthday.
- **Splash Mountain** is also missing from this map, as it wouldn't open in Frontierland until 1992.

This 1986 guide map is a beautiful piece of Disney park history, reflecting the layout and design of an era when Walt Disney World was cementing its place as the vacation capital of the world.
