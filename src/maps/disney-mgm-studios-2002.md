This 2002 park guide map captures **Disney-MGM Studios** during a fascinating era of transition. Originally opened in 1989 as Walt Disney World's third theme park, the park was designed to operate as both a fully functioning production studio and a celebration of classic Hollywood. By 2002, as seen in the map of the park, the park was heavily leaning into its role as an interactive, franchise-driven entertainment hub while celebrating a major milestone: **Walt Disney's 100th Birthday ("100 Years of Magic")**.

![Disney MGM Studios 2002](/assets/img/maps/disney-mgm-studios-2002.JPG)

### A Snapshot of 2002 Nostalgia

Looking closely at the map, you can spot several legendary, extinct attractions that defined this era of the park:

-   **The Sorcerer's Hat:** Dominating the center of the map, this massive icon was installed for the 100 Years of Magic celebration and stood right in front of the *Great Movie Ride* until its removal in 2015.

-   **Who Wants to Be a Millionaire - Play It!:** Located at the top of the map (Attraction 9), this interactive attraction perfectly captured the early-2000s game show craze, allowing guests to sit in a replica "Hot Seat" for a chance to win unique prizes.

-   **Disney Stars and Motor Cars Parade:** Highlighted in the top left, this beloved parade debuted for the celebration, featuring characters riding in highly stylized, custom-designed cars.

-   **Classic "Studio" Elements:** The map proudly features *The Disney-MGM Studios Backlot Tour* (Attraction 8), *Sounds Dangerous---Starring Drew Carey* (Attraction 3), and *The Magic of Disney Animation* (Attraction 12)---remnants of the park's original pitch as a peek behind the Hollywood curtain.

### Thrills, Shows, and the Future Hollywood Studios

Interspersed with these nostalgic favorites are the heavy-hitter attractions that still anchor the park today. On the right side of the map, you can spot the formidable one-two punch of Sunset Boulevard: **The Twilight Zone Tower of Terror** (Attraction 17) and the **Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith** (Attraction 16). At the bottom right, **Fantasmic!** (Attraction 18) is highlighted as the nighttime spectacular.

Today, Disney MGM Studios is named Disney's Hollywood Studios. Ultimately, this map serves as a beautiful time capsule. It represents the exact midpoint of the park's history, long after it ceased being an active film studio, but well before it transformed into the IP-heavy lands of *Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge* and *Toy Story Land* that guests explore today.
