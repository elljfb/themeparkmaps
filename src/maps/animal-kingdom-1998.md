Disney's Animal Kingdom opened on Earth Day, April 22, 1998, introducing guests to a completely new type of theme park that blended live animals, elaborate settings, and dramatic adventures. This 1998 guide map documents the park at its very beginning, highlighting a layout that was vastly different from the other Walt Disney World parks but grounded in the same legendary attention to detail.

![Animal Kingdom Map 1998](/assets/img/maps/animal_kingdom_map_1998.jpg)

### Opening Year Lands and Layout

The 1998 map showcases the five original areas that welcomed guests during the park’s first year:

- **The Oasis** — the lush, tropical entry pathway designed to ease visitors into the sights and sounds of nature, featuring animal exhibits and winding paths.
- **Safari Village** — the park's central hub situated on an island in the Discovery River, anchored by the iconic **Tree of Life** and serving as the gateway to the other lands (later renamed Discovery Island).
- **Camp Minnie-Mickey** — a rustic, woodland area designed to look like a summer camp where visitors could meet classic Disney characters and watch the spectacular **Festival of the Lion King** show.
- **Africa** — featuring the village of Harambe, which served as the entrance to the signature **Kilimanjaro Safaris** expedition across the savanna and the Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail.
- **DinoLand U.S.A.** — themed as a quirky roadside dig site, home to the thrilling **Countdown to Extinction** (later renamed DINOSAUR) and the Boneyard play area.

### An Evolving Adventure

What makes the 1998 Animal Kingdom map so fascinating is what it *doesn't* show. Asia was still under construction (opening later in 1999 with the Kali River Rapids), and the legendary Expedition Everest coaster wouldn't arrive until 2006. 

This map is a wonderful time capsule from the park's early days, capturing the original vision of celebrating animals, nature, and adventure before the park expanded into the world-class destination it is today.
